    “National Treasure: Edge of History” Actress Breeda Wool Wiki | Bio

    Breeda Wool (born on May 2, 1982) is a 40-year-old American actress of Irish roots known for her work in short movies and TV shows. She is Lou Linklatter from the famous TV series “Mr. Mercedes,” where she worked alongside Brendan Gleeson, Holland Taylor, Jharrel Jerome, and Justine Lupe. People also recognize her for the part she played in the Peabody Award winner show “UnREAL,” which aired on Lifetime.

    In 2022, Wool gained publicity after she starred alongside stars like Lisette Olivera, Justin Bartha, and Lyndon Smith in the Disney+ series “National Treasure: Edge of History.” In this Nicholas Cage’s hit film spinoff, Breeda played the role of Kacey, whereas Lisette played the protagonist, a girl named Jess Valenzuela, looking for the solution to a mystery that leads to treasure.     How Old is Breeda Wool?

    On May 2, 1982, Breeda Wool (age 40) took birth in a family of Irish origin in Urbana, Illinois, America. Her father, Richard Wool, was a scientist, and her mother, Deborah Lee Wool, is an artist. She also has two sisters, namely Sorcha and Meghan.

    According to sources, Bree’s journey to becoming an actor started at elementary school. Until high school, she did theatre and got a scholarship from Wagner College, where she majored in Psychology, Philosophy, and Theatre. Despite being a scientist, her dad never questioned his daughter’s career choice and always supported her.

    Following her dream to become an actress, she moved to NYC, where she performed as a street artist and did movement dance for years. Meanwhile, she did a few short movies, including “Black Hole,” “Dandelion Fall,” and “The Masterpiece.”

    Breeda Wool’s Career Timeline

    After spending some time in New York, Wool came to Los Angeles, where she got to the role of Rayna in “AWOL,” a critically acclaimed short movie that earned applause at the Sundance Film Festival. In 2013 she appeared in “Lambing Season,” for which she received Best Actress in a Short Film Award at Woods Hole Film Festival.

    Meanwhile, she kept doing minor roles in TV shows; her first work on small-screen was when she played the part of a woman protester in “Law & Order: Criminal Intent.” After playing uncredited roles for years, in 2016, Breeda received her major break with TV series titled “UnREAL,” where she played Faith Duluth.

    It was a comedy show where director Sarah Gertrude Shapiro filmed Wool alongside Haviland Stillwell, Malea Mitchell, Dot-Marie Jones, and Adam Huber. Following the success of “UnREAL,” Bree starred in “Mr. Mercedes,” “GLOW,” and “Room 104.” You might have also seen her as Aiden in Season 10 of the AMC show “The Walking Dead.” Currently, she is being praised for how well she portrayed Kacey in “National Treasure: Edge of History.”
