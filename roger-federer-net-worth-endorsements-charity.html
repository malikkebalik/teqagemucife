    Roger Federer | Charity

    Federer founded the Roger Federer Foundation in 2003, intending to provide education and sport to underprivileged children.

    Roger and Nadal played against each other in 2010 at Munich and Zurich, raising more than $4 million for the Roger Federer Foundation.

    To date, his foundation has raised more than $50 million and has provided education to more than 1.5 million children in Africa.

    Likewise, he played against Stan Wawrinka in a charity match and raised $1.12 million. The proceeds of the match went to educational projects in Africa.

    Moreover, he and other players held the charity event in Australia Open, “Hit For Haiti,” and raised money that went to earthquake victims of Haiti.

    Federer also participated in the charity exhibition Indian Wells Masters which raised around $1 million.

    Furthermore, he also took part in the event’ Rally For Relief,’ which was initiated to help victims of Queensland floods.

    Roger Federer | Career

    Roger Federer started his tennis career in 1988, playing against Lucas Arnold Ker in Swiss Open Gstaad. After a few more wins, Roger became the youngest member of the ATP’s top 100. 

    Similarly, Roger represented Switzerland at the 2000 Olympics. He reached the semifinals but sadly lost to Tommy Haas and Arnaud Di Pasquale.

    In 2013 Federer struggled to bounce back because of his back injury causing his rankings to plummet. Moreover, in 2020 Federer lost to Djokovic in 2020 Australian Open and also sustained a knee injury.

    He eventually withdrew from further competitions, citing needing time for his knee to recover. Furthermore, he has a winning record of 82%, with 1251 wins and 275 losses.

    Federer has appeared in singles and doubles competitions, winning various Grand Slams titles like Australian Open, Wimbledon, French Open, and US Open.
