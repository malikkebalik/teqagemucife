    Introduction 

    Retired German race car driver, Sebastian Vettel, won the World Drivers’ Championship at the youngest age of any driver in history, as well as becoming the youngest double, treble, and quadruple world champion in Formula One.

    Previously, he raced for Red Bull, Toro Rosso, BMW, and Ferrari Scuderia and is one of only four drivers who have won four or more championships in Formula One.

    As of February 2024, Sebastian Vettel’s net worth is estimated to be $140 Million.

    Early Life 

    Sebastion Vettel was born July 3, 1987, in Heppenheim, Germany.

    Vettel’s parents are Norbert and Heike Vettel and he has a younger brother, Fabian, who is also a racing driver, and two elder sisters, Melanie and Stefanie.

    At the age of 4, Sebastian Vettel began amateur karting, and at the age of 8, he started competing in a kart series.

    He was accepted into the Red Bull Junior Team when he was eleven and, in 2001, he won the Junior Monaco Kart Cup, among other junior championships.

    Career 

    Sebastian Vettel began driving open-wheel racing cars in 2003 and won the German Formula BMW Championship in 2004 with 18 wins from 20 races.

    He was promoted as a test driver for Sauber in Formula One in 2006 when he was only 18 years old and was able to win a few races, but not the title.

    At the 2006 Grand Prix, he made history by becoming the sport’s newest driver – recording the top time in Friday practice during the second testing for the 2006 Italian Grand Prix.

    He took part in the US Grand Prix, earned his first World Championship point, and set a record for the youngest Formula One driver to earn a point.

    His first victory of the 2013 season came in Malaysia, where he also finished second to Michael Schumacher’s single-season record with 13 victories.

    With three rounds remaining, he finally won the drivers’ championship for a fourth straight time.

    2014, however, was not a lucky year for him – he placed fifth in the standings despite not having any victories.

    According to experts, he was unable to change his driving style to comply with the new technical standards and took Fernando Alonso’s seat in the 2015 season at Ferrari Scuderia.

    The test was successful because he won three Grand Prix races in the first season, defying his critics and silencing the doubting fans.

    Vettel concluded the 2018 season with five race victories but finished second to Lewis Hamilton, who won the World Drivers’ Championship for a second straight year.
