    Kadeena Cox is a British parasport athlete and television presenter. She boasts five remarkable Paralympic gold medals, triumphing in athletics and cycling at the 2016 Summer Paralympics.

    Similarly, she became the first British Paralympian since Isabel Barr in 1984 to secure gold in multiple sports at the same Games.

    Cox received a multiple sclerosis (MS) diagnosis in 2013. In 2015, she entered the world of parasports, swiftly ascending to the pinnacle of her discipline.

    Her achievements include clinching the gold medal in the T37 100m at the 2015 IPC Athletics World Championships and the C4 500m time trial at the 2016 UCI Para-cycling Track World Championships.

    Participating in both athletics and cycling at the 2016 Summer Paralympics, Cox earned a bronze in the Women’s 100m T38 sprint.

    Subsequently, she secured gold in the Women’s C4-5 cycling time trial and the T38 400m sprint.
